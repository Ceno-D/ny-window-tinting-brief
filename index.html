What We Found
The Account Had 3 Problems Killing Performance
After a full 74-check audit across every campaign, ad group, keyword, creative asset, and landing page.
Critical
🎯
Near Me RSA: Wrong Headline Pinned
H1 was pinned to "Long Island Window Tinting" — a geographic headline — but every keyword in the ad group is a proximity query ("tint shop near me", "car tints near me"). Google tanks Quality Score when the headline doesn't match intent. This is why 5 keywords were flagged "low quality."
Critical
💸
One Keyword Burning $36 With Zero Returns
"car window tinting near me" (Broad Match) — 8 clicks, $36.28 spent, 0 conversions, "Low Quality" flag. This single keyword was consuming 65% of the Near Me campaign's entire budget while producing nothing.
Critical
📦
PMax Running on Stock Images — No Real Photos
All 20 images in the PMax asset group were auto-generated stock URLs from Google's syndication network. Jerry's real shop photos, the Corvette, the Tesla — none of it was in the ads. Google was showing generic imagery to represent a 40-year-old certified shop.
Warning
⚡
PMax Capped at $15/Day — Limited by Budget Daily
The PMax campaign hits its daily cap every morning. At $38 CPA and $15/day, the algorithm gets 0.4 conversions/day ceiling — then stops showing ads. The 14-day CPA jumped to $66 because budget-capped PMax can't optimize effectively.
Warning
📝
Both Active RSAs: POOR Ad Strength
The rebuilt ads had redundant headlines (3 XPEL references, 2 "40 years" references) and 2 pinned headlines each — leaving only 1 free position for Google to optimize. Google flagged both as POOR and couldn't effectively A/B test combinations.
Warning
🌐
Landing Page: 84 Reviews Hidden, 11 H1 Tags
The ad landing page has a "See What Our Customers Say" section that exists but shows no reviews. Jerry has 84 Google reviews at 4.8★ — none appear on the page. Meanwhile, 11 separate sections are all tagged H1, suppressing Google's landing page experience score.
Strong
✅
Conversion Tracking: Clean
All conversions are real phone calls from ads. No view-through inflation, no duplicate conversion actions. The tracking foundation is solid — data can be trusted for optimization decisions.
Strong
💡
Local Campaign: Exceptional CVR
The Local ad group is converting at 28.57% — nearly 4× the industry average for local services. When someone clicks, they call. The landing page and offer are working. The problem is volume, not quality.
Strong
🚫
Negative Keywords: Solid Foundation
Two manager-owned shared lists (780+ and 164 terms) are active, plus campaign-level negatives covering competitor names, exemption queries, wrong locations, and Spanish language queries. Clean from the Phase 1 work.
